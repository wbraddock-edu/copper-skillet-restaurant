Our Story

Cooking the way Grandma taught us.

Bobby Ray Harmon grew up in the kitchen — not just any kitchen, but his grandmother Nell's. Every Saturday morning meant cast iron skillets sizzling on a wood-burning stove, the smell of biscuits rising in the oven, and a pot of something long-simmered on the back burner. Those mornings shaped everything.

After years working the line at restaurants in Roanoke, Bobby Ray came home to Pulaski in 2009 with a dream, three generations of handwritten recipes, and just enough savings to lease a little corner spot on Washington Avenue. Fifteen years later, The Copper Skillet has served over 200,000 meals — and the gravy recipe still hasn't changed.
